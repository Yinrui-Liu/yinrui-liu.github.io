I am passionate about science outreach. I have participated in numerous public science events (shout out to the South Side Science Festival in Chicago). I've always enjoyed sharing and hearing about others' research.

Reflecting on my own childhood, I did not have access to many local outreach resources. With the hope of extending science communication from local communities to a global audience, I launched an online video programüçÄ, where we aim to involve PhD students from various fields, ranging from science to humanities, to introduce their research in a way that is accessible and engaging to the general public. I gathered a group of volunteers from diverse scientific backgrounds, including PhDs and students specialized in video production. We are scheduled to start releasing videos on platforms including YouTube early next year. That said, I can share our pilot video about neutrino physics, my own research, below. (I provided scripts and voiceover in Mandarin, but English subtitles are always available. In case it doesn't play properly, you can view it on Google Drive or YouTube (unlisted).)
